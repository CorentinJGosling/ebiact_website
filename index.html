Welcome to the EBIA-CT database!

The evidence-based interventions for Autism: clinical trials (EBIA-CT) database allows to access information on meta-analyses and individual clinical trials exploring the efficacy of interventions in people with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

How this database has been created?

Our team has reviewed the scientific literature in a systematic manner to locate any meta-analysis of controlled clinical trials exploring the efficacy of interventions in people with ASD.

For now, we have focused on the efficacy of psychosocial interventions. If you are interested in obtaining more information on the interventions described in the database, a brief description of each intervention is available in the "Intervention description" section.

How this database is working?

We have extracted critical information from identified meta-analyses (such as characteristics of included participants, type of intervention assessed, clinical outcome targeted by the intervention, efficacy and risk of bias of included clinical trials). Using this information, we have replicated meta-analytic calculations to determine the efficacy of the different types of interventions on core and related ASD symptoms. Finally, we have assessed the methodological quality of the meta-analyses to present information coming from the most reliable and up-to-date scientific studies.

Precise methodology employed by our team can be retrieved in scientific articles published along with this database. These papers are freely accessible in the "Publications" section of this website.

All our data and results have then been stored (and can be downloaded) in an online database so that everyone can access the results of our work. We have implanted an online filtering system to faciliate searches for precise information about a specific intervention, outcome or population.

How to access this database?

Simply click on the "Database" section of this website, everyting is open-access!

I know a clinical trial that is not listed in the database or I have additional information about a clinical trial already included in the database.

Many thanks for noticing this! If you want to provide more information about a trial, simply download this form and send it back to us completed at corentin.gosling@parisnanterre.fr.

Your work will be acknowledged in the "About us" section. If you provide information about one trial, you (or your team) will appear as a 'Contributor'. If you provide information on many trials, you will appear as a 'Significant contributor'.

I noticed an error in the information stored about a clinical trial.

We are sorry for this mistake. Please, provide a proof that the information stored in the database is erroneous as well as corrected information and email it to us at corentin.gosling@parisnanterre.fr.

Your work will be acknowledged in the "About us" section, in which you will appear as a 'Contributor' for a minor correction or as a 'Debunker' for a major one.
