Description of L-carnosine

L-carnosine is a dipeptide molecule composed of the amino acids beta-alanine and histidine. It is found in high concentrations in muscle tissue and the brain, playing a crucial role in cellular health and function. L-carnosine acts as a potent antioxidant, neutralizing harmful free radicals and protecting cells from oxidative stress.Beyond its antioxidative properties, recent research has highlighted L-carnosine's involvement in various physiological processes, including protein homeostasis, cellular repair, and immune regulation. These functions contribute to its importance in maintaining overall health and preventing age-related decline.

Given its role in cellular protection and repair, L-carnosine has been investigated for its potential benefits in a range of health conditions, including neurodegenerative diseases, cardiovascular health, and metabolic disorders. It has also been explored for its possible therapeutic effects in Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

Main features of L-carnosine
Users of L-CARNO
The intervention is typically designed for School-age children (6-12 yo).
Implementation
This type of intervention is delivered at home. It is typically administered by professionals.
Duration and dosage
On average this type of intervention lasts about 3 months (from 2 months to 6 months), for an average of 931 mg/day (from 225 mg/day to 2200 mg/day).
