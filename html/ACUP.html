Description of ACUP

Acupuncture is a form of traditional Chinese medicine, and the most commonly used CAM in China for children with ASD (40% in Hong Kong) (Wang, 2021). The treatment consists of stimulating the energy channels (meridians) by placing needles close to the tissues at specific points (400 "acupoints" in total). There are several techniques: needling, moxibustion (heating the acupoints), laser, electrical stimulation (electro-acupuncture). The technique can be performed on the whole body, on the skull, or on the tongue. The traditional model is based on a holistic approach to the human being, aiming at a balance between mind and body (homeostasis). According to this model, ASD is the result of "dysregulation and insufficiency of the brain and mind" (Chen et al., 2008, cited by Cheuk, 2011). Stimulation would then alleviate the symptoms by reharmonising body and mind. The mechanisms possibly at work in the treatment of ASD would be based on the modulation of neurotransmitters, glutamate and GABA, whose functioning is disrupted in ASD according to certain studies (Chao 2010, Carlson 2011, cited by Cheuk, 2011). The medical acupuncture paradigm hypothesises that 'acupoints' are linked to sensory neurons. Stimulation would then trigger a cascade of neurological responses to central nervous system structures, with effects on endorphin, serotonin and endocannabinoid production, immunological and endocrinological markers.
