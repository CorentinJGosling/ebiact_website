Description of N-acetylcysteine

N-acetylcysteine (NAC) is a derivative of the amino acid cysteine and serves as a precursor to glutathione, one of the body’s most essential antioxidants. Glutathione plays a critical role in protecting cells from oxidative stress and maintaining overall cellular health. Due to its powerful antioxidant properties and its ability to modulate the glutamatergic and dopaminergic systems, NAC has been explored as a therapeutic option for various psychiatric and neurological conditions.

Main features of N-acetylcysteine
Users of NAC
The intervention is typically designed for School-age children (6-12 yo), with important cognitive difficulties.
Implementation
This type of intervention is delivered at home. It is typically administered by professionals.
Duration and dosage
On average this type of intervention lasts about 3 months (from 2 months to 6 months), for an average of 1186 mg/day (from 500 mg/day to 1800 mg/day).
